History of Capitalist Development - Introduction

In 2007, the world passed a momentous milestone. In that year, for the first time in human history, the number of people living in cities exceeded that of the countryside.1 The enormity of this development is hard to overstate. As recently as 1960, the global rural population was double that of the urban.2 Return to the 19th century, and in most areas the non-agricultural population was vanishingly small.
